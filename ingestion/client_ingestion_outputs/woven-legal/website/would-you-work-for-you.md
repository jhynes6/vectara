# Would You Work for You?

![](https://wovenlegal.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/image.png)

by [Patti Cotter](https://wovenlegal.com/author/patti-cotter/)

on [September 17, 2025](https://wovenlegal.com/2025/09/17/)

## _üö©_ **6 Red Flags That Tell Candidates Your Firm Is Dysfunctional**

When an attorney tells me they‚Äôre frustrated by turnover or struggling to hire top talent, especially when hiring paralegals, I have been known to ask a blunt question: Would you work for you?

It‚Äôs not meant to be flippant‚Äîit‚Äôs a lens for assessing not just business practices, but also the attitude and respect an attorney brings to their team.

Because here‚Äôs the truth: candidates have already checked the basics. They know your salary range. They‚Äôve compared your benefits package. If they‚Äôre still sitting in the interview, they‚Äôve decided you‚Äôre at least competitive on paper. But that‚Äôs just the baseline. The real decision about whether to join your firm comes down to culture, behavior, and how you run the place day-to-day.

And this is where firms lose great people. The polished outside doesn‚Äôt match the messy inside. The red flags pop up early‚Äîand talented candidates take note.

* * *

Here are six of the biggest ones I see that make candidates decide your firm isn‚Äôt worth it:

### **1\. You‚Äôre Late, Distracted, or Multitasking During Interviews**

It may sound small, but it‚Äôs huge. When you‚Äôre late for the interview, accept calls during the conversation, or start typing while the candidate is speaking (and it‚Äôs obvious you‚Äôre not taking notes), you send a loud message: I don‚Äôt value your time.

Respect is the foundation of any healthy work relationship. If you‚Äôre careless with it in the very first interaction, a strong candidate assumes that lack of respect carries through everything else‚Äîdeadlines, communication, boundaries, and support.

**Why it matters: You‚Äôve spent years building a reputation as a serious professional. But to the person across the table, that reputation collapses the second you act like their time is disposable.**

**[How to fix it:](https://wovenlegal.com/ai-virtualassistants-productivityhacks-what-works-what-doesnt/)** Treat interviews like client meetings. Block your calendar. Silence notifications. Show up on time, prepared, and fully engaged. The way you handle this 45-minute conversation tells candidates exactly how you‚Äôll handle them as part of your team. Learn more about effective hiring for law firms.

### **2\. You Criticize Past Employees**

It happens more often than you‚Äôd think: an attorney explains the open position by blaming the last person who held it. ‚ÄúShe just wasn‚Äôt reliable.‚Äù ‚ÄúHe didn‚Äôt have the drive.‚Äù ‚ÄúThey weren‚Äôt a good cultural fit.‚Äù

Here‚Äôs the problem: every time you talk badly about a former employee, candidates see their own future. If you‚Äôre quick to trash someone once they‚Äôve left, they know you‚Äôll do the same to them. Worse, they‚Äôll assume the turnover isn‚Äôt about the past hire‚Äîit‚Äôs about you.

**Why it matters: Great candidates are allergic to toxic environments. Blame culture is the fastest way to show them the exit door.**

**How to fix it:** Frame turnover in terms of what you‚Äôve learned, not what they lacked. Say, ‚ÄúWe realized this role requires more initiative, so we‚Äôre now looking for someone who thrives on autonomy.‚Äù That shifts the conversation from blame to growth. ### **3\. Your Tech and Processes Are Outdated‚Äîor Nonexistent**

For attorneys, legal knowledge is the craft. For staff, tools and processes are the craft. If you hand them an outdated case management system (or worse‚Äînone at all), no documented workflows, and an inbox full of bottlenecks, you‚Äôre telling them their time will be wasted.

Talented professionals want to practice their skills, not babysit bad tech or reinvent the wheel every week.

**Why it matters: A lack of infrastructure screams ‚Äúchaos.‚Äù Candidates equate chaos with burnout, and burnout with exit. A 2025 Clio Legal Trends Report found 80% of solo and small firm lawyers use cloud-based legal practice management software for efficiency.**

**How to fix it:** Audit your tools. If you‚Äôre running on spreadsheets and sticky notes, upgrade. If you‚Äôve got software but no one knows how to use it, invest in training and process design. Nothing says ‚Äúwe‚Äôre serious about growth‚Äù like running a firm that actually works. Explore our virtual paralegal solutions to streamline operations.

### **4\. You Bring Up Politics in the Interview**

There are few faster ways to alienate a candidate than dropping political commentary in the first conversation. It signals that your worldview bleeds into the workplace, and that every decision, interaction, or conflict may be judged through that lens.

Even if you think the candidate ‚Äúagrees with you,‚Äù they‚Äôre silently thinking: If this is how we start, what happens when I don‚Äôt agree?

**Why it matters: Politics polarize. Law firms require collaboration. If you suggest‚Äîintentionally or not‚Äîthat political alignment is part of the culture, you‚Äôve already cut your candidate pool in half and undermined trust before it starts.**

**How to fix it:** Keep interviews professional. Candidates want to know they‚Äôll be valued for their expertise, not their voting record. That‚Äôs not ‚Äúplaying it safe‚Äù‚Äîthat‚Äôs leadership. See how Woven ensures bias-free candidate matching.

### **5\. You‚Äôre a Micromanager**

If there‚Äôs one red flag that repels great candidates more than any other, it‚Äôs micromanagement. It‚Äôs the most prevalent and the most damaging.

Hovering, second-guessing, and nitpicking every task communicates distrust. Distrust suffocates initiative. Once initiative dies, talent walks out the door.

Micromanagers often tell me, ‚ÄúI just want things done right.‚Äù But what they‚Äôre really saying is, ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt trust anyone but me to get it right.‚Äù That mindset may feel protective, but it‚Äôs poison for retention. People quit managers, not jobs‚Äîand in case you‚Äôve forgotten, unsolicited advice is just another form of criticism.

**Why it matters: No top-tier professional is going to stay where they feel infantilized. A Thomson Reuters report noted associate turnover peaked at 24% in 2021, often due to factors like lack of autonomy, with ongoing concerns in later years.**

**[How to fix it:](https://wovenlegal.com/attorney-delegation-worksheet/)** Shift from control to accountability. Define outcomes clearly. Set check-in points. Then back off and let your team deliver. You‚Äôll be surprised how often they exceed your expectations when you stop clipping their wings. Learn about empowering legal teams.

### **6\. It‚Äôs Obvious You Have a ‚ÄúPet‚Äù Employee**

Candidates notice fast when one person in the firm is treated as the favorite. And it‚Äôs not about recognizing talent‚Äîevery team has high performers. It‚Äôs about how that favoritism plays out in the culture.

If your ‚Äúgo-to‚Äù person is elevated in a way that sidelines or undermines others, candidates see the politics instantly. They assume growth opportunities will be blocked and that respect won‚Äôt be distributed fairly.

**Why it matters: Nobody wants to walk into a workplace where one person is above reproach and everyone else is expendable. That dynamic kills collaboration and accelerates turnover.**

**The exception: Having a trusted employee isn‚Äôt inherently bad‚Äîif that person models professionalism, compassion, and leadership. The right ‚Äúgo-to‚Äù person calms storms, speaks hard truths aligned with your expectations, and pays their experience forward without feeling threatened by peers. In that case, they elevate the culture instead of distorting it.**

**How to fix it:** If you have a favorite, ask yourself: does this person empower the team or intimidate them? If it‚Äôs the latter, you‚Äôre not just hurting morale‚Äîyou‚Äôre shrinking your hiring pool. Discover how Woven‚Äôs virtual teams enhance collaboration.

## **Final Thought: Culture Always Shows**

Every candidate comes to the table assuming the basics‚Äîsalary, benefits, workload‚Äîare in order. What makes or breaks their decision isn‚Äôt compensation. It‚Äôs whether they believe your firm is a place where they‚Äôll be respected, equipped, trusted, and valued.

If you‚Äôre struggling with retention, stop asking what‚Äôs wrong with the talent pool and start asking the tougher question: **Would you work for YOU?**

Because if the answer is no, the right candidate will spot it long before you do. And they‚Äôll move on to a firm where the insides actually match the outsides.
